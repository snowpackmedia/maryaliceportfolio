The Miracle Worker - Westlake High School

The Miracle Worker is a play about Helen Keller, her family and Anne Sullivan, the teacher who gave her hope. This performance was put on by the drama department at Westlake High School in Saratoga Springs, Utah.

This show was put on during the height of the COVID pandemic, which presented a unique challenge. Budgets for this play had been decimated so the play was put on with a shoestring budget and a smaller cast. However, like many plays at Westlake, this play was still double cast to give more students the opportunity to partipate. I had to work hard to maintain the overall look and feel of the costumes in the show while allowing for the differing body types of the role players.

My visions for the costumes in the play was to start the family and Helen in darker clothing at the beginning symbolizing the dark place they were in. As the show progresses, the costumes get lighter symbolizing the hope that comes into her life as she learns to commuicate with her family. The crones in the show were costumed with my vison of spectral beings working to steal hope and sanity.
